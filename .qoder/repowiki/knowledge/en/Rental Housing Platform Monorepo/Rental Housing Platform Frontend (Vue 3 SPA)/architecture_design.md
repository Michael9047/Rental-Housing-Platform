The app is bootstrapped in `src/main.ts` by mounting one Vue app with three cross-cutting plugins — Pinia for global state (`auth`, `property`), vue-router for navigation, and Element Plus (Chinese locale) with all icons registered globally. All routes are declared centrally in `src/router/index.ts` under a `DefaultLayout` shell; route-level `meta.requiresAuth / requiresLandlord / requiresAdmin / guest` flags drive a single `beforeEach` guard that reads `access_token` and `user` from `localStorage` and redirects unauthenticated or unauthorized users. Views (tenant search/discovery, booking/payment flow, landlord dashboard, AI search, auth/profile, plus `views/admin/*`) are pure presenters that call domain-scoped services in `src/services/` (e.g. `auth.ts`, `booking.ts`, `property.ts`, `admin.ts`). Shared reusable UI lives in `src/components/` (PropertyCard, AmapMap, BookingDateDialog, GlobalFooter) and in `src/components/uhomes/` for design-tokens-driven Element Plus overrides. Cross-view state is kept in Pinia stores under `src/stores/`; per-route TypeScript contracts live in `src/types/`. The build pipeline (`vite dev` / `vue-tsc && vite build`) targets a static bundle served by the sibling `nginx/nginx.conf` container.
